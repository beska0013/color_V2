Retinal Vascular Diseases Virtual Clinic

Retinal vascular diseases are a leading cause of blindness. Patients with these conditions are often underassessed, underdiagnosed, and/or not sufficiently treated. These may be explained by patients’ lack of awareness of disease progression, as well as barriers to accessibility, and lack of treatment compliance. The COVID-19 pandemic has contributed to worsening accessibility of healthcare and hence the visual outcomes of those affected. However, it has also prompted changes around digital healthcare to overcome existing limitations in retinal vascular disease management. Together with advances in artificial intelligence, new technologies are speeding up the diagnosis and referral of patients with retinal vascular diseases, easing the burden on hospitals and clinicians.

In this Podcast series on retinal vascular diseases, provided by Howitreat.MD by SEI Healthcare, four renowned world clinical experts will address the issues surrounding diagnosis and treatment of patients with neovascular age-related macular degeneration (nAMD), diabetic retinopathy (DR), and diabetic macular edema (DME), disease management through home monitoring, and the role of artificial intelligence.

Learning Goal / Purpose

The goal of this activity is to educate ophthalmologists on various topics on disease management optimization of nAMD and DME.

Target Audience

This activity is intended for ophthalmologists in Europe.

Objectives

After completing this educational activity, the participant will be able to:

  • Identify the main barriers for an efficient screening, diagnosis, and treatment of people with nAMD, DR/DME and the role of new technologies to overcome them

  • List the current treatments for retinal vascular diseases, including nAMD and DME, and differentiate their relative effectiveness and limitations, as well as recognize unmet medical needs that are still to be covered by drugs currently under development

  • Recognize the negative impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on delaying disease diagnosis and management, and recognize how it has also prompted changes around digital healthcare to overcome these issues

  • Identify the current status of AI in ophthalmology and recognize the potential of AI to revolutionize the management of retinal vascular diseases, including applications of disease stratification by image analyses and home monitoring

  • Identify the additional value of biosimilar use in more unexperienced clinical specialties, such as ophthalmology

  • Describe the process of the validation of AI technology in ophthalmology before its implementation in clinical practice

  • Describe biosimilarity concept, list the regulatory requirements and identify the main similarities and differences between the regulatory frameworks in Europe versus the United States
